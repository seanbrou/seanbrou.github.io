Our Story

A little place with a whole lot of heart.

Pop's Diner started the way most great diners do — with a dream, a cast-iron skillet, and a belief that a good meal can turn anybody's day around. Back in the early days, Pop himself would be here before the sun came up, brewing the first pot of coffee and getting the biscuits in the oven. He wanted a place where folks could pull off Old Highway 46, sit down in a booth that felt like home, and eat a meal that tasted like someone who loved them made it. That spirit hasn't changed one bit.

Today, Pop's is more than a restaurant — it's where Greensburg gathers. You'll find farmers finishing up early mornings next to families spending Saturday morning together. Regulars who've been coming for decades still sit at the same stools, and newcomers are always welcomed like old friends. We still make our pie from scratch, still pour coffee until your cup runs dry, and still believe there's nothing a warm plate and a kind word can't fix. Pull up a chair. You're always welcome here.
